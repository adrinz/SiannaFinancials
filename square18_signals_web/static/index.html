Market regime A single-label summary of today's tape, blending VIX level, breadth (% of stocks above their 50-day MA), put/call ratio, and a composite trend score. Possible labels: Risk-On — buyers in control, broad participation. Risk-Off — volatility expanding, breadth thin, hedges bid. Mixed — indicators disagree; trade smaller size. The qualifier (narrow / broad) flags how many stocks are participating in the move. The screener uses this to dial position size up or down across the universe.
VIX —   VIX — 30-day implied volatility Expected 1-sigma move in the S&P 500 over the next month, derived from SPX option prices. Rule of thumb: < 15 calm, 15–20 normal, 20–30 elevated, > 30 panic. Higher VIX = wider expected moves and more expensive options. The % in parentheses is today's change in the VIX itself. · breadth — above 50d Breadth — % above 50-day MA Share of tracked stocks trading above their own 50-day simple moving average. It's the single cleanest measure of participation: > 70% overheated, 50–70% healthy uptrend, 30–50% weakening, < 30% washout territory. A rally with breadth < 40% is usually carried by a handful of mega-caps and is fragile. · put/call — Put/call ratio Total put option volume divided by total call option volume. A contrarian sentiment gauge: < 0.70 complacent / greedy, 0.70–1.00 balanced, > 1.00 defensive / fearful. Extreme readings in either direction often precede mean reversion. Source: CBOE equity + index options. · trend score — Composite trend score Our internal market-state score on a −1 to +1 scale, blending SPY's position vs. 20/50/200-day MAs, slope of the 50-day, sector dispersion, and new-high / new-low ratio. > +0.3 bullish trend, −0.3 to +0.3 chop, < −0.3 bearish trend. Drives position sizing: bullish trend ⇒ longs get full size, shorts get half; bearish ⇒ the reverse.

Longs — Buy signals Tickers whose composite technical score crosses the bullish threshold (trend + momentum + volume confirmation ≥ 0.25) with positive conviction. These are the setups the screener would long today. Click the Buy pill below to see them.

Shorts i
Shorts — Sell signals Tickers whose composite score is bearish (≤ −0.25) with negative conviction — downtrend intact, momentum rolling over, and volume confirming. Candidates for long puts or outright shorts. Click the Sell pill below to see them.

Hold / skip i
Hold / skip Tickers whose score sits in the neutral zone (−0.25 < score < 0.25) or whose conviction is too low to justify risking capital. The screener explicitly passes on these — no position today. "Do nothing" is a valid trade.

VIX i
VIX — CBOE Volatility Index Market's 30-day forward expectation of S&P 500 volatility, derived from SPX option prices. Rough rule of thumb: < 15 calm, 15–20 normal, 20–30 elevated, > 30 panic. High VIX expands option premiums and widens expected moves. Also called the "fear gauge."

Breadth up i
Breadth up — % of tickers green today Percentage of the tracked universe trading above their prior close right now. A healthy rally has broad participation (> 60%); a rally led by 2–3 mega-caps while breadth is < 40% is fragile. Recomputed on every 5-minute refresh.
